<p><p>High Performance Liquid Chromatography (HPLC) is a technique used to separate, identify, and quantify components in a mixture. The market encompasses various types, including partition chromatography for separating based on solubility, normal-phase and reversed-phase chromatography, which differentiate compounds by polarity, and displacement chromatography that enhances separation efficiency. Size-exclusion chromatography separates based on molecular size, while ion-exchange chromatography targets ionic interactions. Bioaffinity chromatography focuses on specific biological interactions, catering to diverse applications in pharmaceuticals, environmental testing, and biotechnology.</p></p>
