<p><p>Catgut Chromic Suture is a type of absorbable surgical suture made from the submucosal layer of sheep intestines or the serosal layer of cattle intestines. It is treated with chromic salts to enhance its strength and delay absorption, making it suitable for wound closure in various surgical procedures. The chromic treatment reduces tissue reaction and extends the suture's lifespan in the body, typically lasting 10 to 14 days before absorption begins. It is commonly used in gynecology, orthopedics, and general surgery due to its biocompatibility and ease of handling.</p><p>The Catgut Chromic Suture Market is experiencing steady growth, driven by increasing surgical procedures, advancements in healthcare infrastructure, and rising demand for absorbable sutures.
