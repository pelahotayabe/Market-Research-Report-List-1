<p><p>The Bone-anchored Hearing System (BAHS) market is divided into implantable and non-implantable segments. Implantable BAHS involves surgical placement of devices that use bone conduction to transmit sound directly to the inner ear, offering a permanent solution for individuals with conductive hearing loss. Non-implantable options, like adhesive or headband devices, provide temporary support and can be easily removed. Both segments serve distinct needs, with implantable systems favored for long-term use and non-implantable systems appealing for their convenience and adaptability.</p></p>
