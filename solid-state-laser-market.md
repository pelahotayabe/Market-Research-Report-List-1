<p><p>The solid-state laser market comprises two primary types: Continuous Wave (CW) lasers and Pulsed Solid State lasers. CW lasers emit a constant beam of light, ideal for applications requiring steady output, such as cutting and medical procedures. In contrast, Pulsed Solid State lasers release energy in short, high-intensity bursts, making them suitable for tasks like material marking and precision welding. Both types serve diverse industries, including manufacturing, healthcare, and telecommunications, leveraging the unique advantages of their operational characteristics.</p></p>
