<p><p>The breast cancer imaging market comprises two primary technology types: ionizing and non-ionizing technologies. Ionizing technologies include methods like mammography and computed tomography (CT), which utilize X-rays to produce images of breast tissue but carry radiation exposure risks. Non-ionizing technologies, such as ultrasound and magnetic resonance imaging (MRI), do not involve radiation and offer safer alternatives for diagnosis and monitoring. Each technology plays a vital role in early detection, treatment planning, and ongoing management of breast cancer.</p></p>
