<p><p>The inorganic oxygen scavenger market comprises three main types: iron-based, sulfite-based, and hydrogenation catalysts. Iron-based scavengers utilize iron compounds to effectively remove oxygen, commonly used in packaging and food preservation. Sulfite-based scavengers employ sodium or potassium sulfites to neutralize oxygen, often seen in beverage and pharmaceutical applications. Hydrogenation catalysts facilitate the conversion of oxygen into harmless compounds, enhancing shelf life and stability in various chemical processes. Each type plays a crucial role in prolonging product longevity and maintaining quality.</p></p>
