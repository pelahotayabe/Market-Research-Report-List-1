<p><p>The Articaine Hydrochloride market can be divided into two primary types: infiltration and nerve block anesthesia. Infiltration is used for localized anesthesia, allowing for numbing specific areas during dental procedures, making it ideal for minor surgeries. Nerve block anesthesia, on the other hand, involves targeting specific nerves to provide more profound anesthesia, effective for larger areas or more invasive procedures. Both types enhance patient comfort and are essential tools in dental and surgical practices, driving demand in the pharmaceutical market.</p></p>
